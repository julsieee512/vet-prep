# Physiology

## Cardiovascular System

### Cardiac Cycle
- **Systole**: ventricular contraction → ejection of blood
- **Diastole**: ventricular relaxation → filling
- **Cardiac output (CO)** = Heart Rate (HR) × Stroke Volume (SV)
- Normal CO dog: ~100 mL/kg/min

### Frank-Starling Law
Increased preload (end-diastolic volume) → increased myocardial stretch → increased force of contraction → increased stroke volume.
- Clinical relevance: IV fluids increase preload → increase CO (up to a point — beyond leads to pulmonary edema)

### Pressure Values (Dog, approximate)
| Location | Systolic | Diastolic |
|---|---|---|
| Left ventricle | 120 mmHg | 5 mmHg |
| Aorta | 120 mmHg | 80 mmHg |
| Right ventricle | 25 mmHg | 5 mmHg |
| Pulmonary artery | 25 mmHg | 10 mmHg |

## Renal Physiology

### Nephron Function by Segment
| Segment | Main Function |
|---|---|
| Proximal convoluted tubule (PCT) | Bulk reabsorption: Na+, glucose, amino acids, HCO3- (~67% of filtrate) |
| Loop of Henle (descending) | Water reabsorption (permeable to water, not solutes) |
| Loop of Henle (ascending) | Na+/K+/2Cl- cotransport — impermeable to water — builds medullary gradient |
| Distal convoluted tubule (DCT) | Na+ reabsorption (aldosterone-regulated) |
| Collecting duct | Water reabsorption (ADH-regulated via aquaporin-2) |

### GFR (Glomerular Filtration Rate)
- Driven by net filtration pressure (Starling forces at glomerulus)
- Estimated by creatinine clearance
- Decreased in: dehydration, hypotension, CKD, NSAID use

### Renal Thresholds
- **Glucose**: ~10 mmol/L → glycosuria above this (SGLT2 saturation)
- **Protein**: trace amounts normal; proteinuria = pathologic if persistent

## Endocrinology

### Hypothalamic-Pituitary Axis
```
Hypothalamus → releasing hormones → Anterior pituitary → tropic hormones → Target gland → hormones → negative feedback
```

### Key Hormones
| Hormone | Source | Target | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| ADH (Vasopressin) | Posterior pituitary | Collecting duct, vessels | Water retention, vasoconstriction |
| Aldosterone | Adrenal cortex (zona glomerulosa) | DCT/collecting duct | Na+ retention, K+ excretion |
| Cortisol | Adrenal cortex (zona fasciculata) | Many tissues | Gluconeogenesis, anti-inflammatory, stress |
| Insulin | Pancreatic β cells | Liver, muscle, fat | Glucose uptake, anabolism |
| Glucagon | Pancreatic α cells | Liver | Glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis |
| PTH | Parathyroid | Bone, kidney | ↑Ca2+, ↓PO4 |
| Calcitonin | Parafollicular (C) cells | Bone | ↓Ca2+ |

## Acid-Base Balance

### Henderson-Hasselbalch
pH = 6.1 + log([HCO3-] / 0.03 × PaCO2)

### Primary Disturbances
| Disturbance | pH | PaCO2 | HCO3- | Compensation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Metabolic acidosis | ↓ | ↓ (compensate) | ↓ | Hyperventilation |
| Metabolic alkalosis | ↑ | ↑ (compensate) | ↑ | Hypoventilation |
| Respiratory acidosis | ↓ | ↑ | ↑ (compensate) | Renal HCO3- retention |
| Respiratory alkalosis | ↑ | ↓ | ↓ (compensate) | Renal HCO3- excretion |

### Anion Gap
AG = Na+ - (Cl- + HCO3-)  
Normal: 12-24 mEq/L (species-dependent)  
**High AG metabolic acidosis** (MUDPILES): Methanol, Uremia, DKA, Propylene glycol, Isoniazid/Iron, Lactic acidosis, Ethylene glycol, Salicylates

## Ruminant-Specific Physiology

### Rumen Fermentation
- VFAs produced: acetate (60%), propionate (20%), butyrate (15%)
- **Propionate** → gluconeogenesis (main glucose precursor in ruminants)
- **Acetate and butyrate** → ketone bodies, fat synthesis
- Rumen pH: 6.0–7.0 (drops in SARA — subacute ruminal acidosis)

### Negative Energy Balance (NEB) in Dairy Cows
- Peak lactation demand exceeds dietary intake
- Mobilization of body fat → NEFA (non-esterified fatty acids) → liver
- Excessive NEFA → ketogenesis → **ketosis** (acetonemia)
- Key ketone bodies: acetoacetate, β-hydroxybutyrate (BHB), acetone
